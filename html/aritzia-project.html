TIMELINE

4 Week Undergrad Project

CONTEXT

Using industry precedent, I was in charge of researching an evidence-based rationale and executing for our design intervention for Aritzia.

PROBLEM

Customers found it difficult to start building a wardrobe that suits their wants and needs.

I initially recognized the disconnect between the customer and shopping experience which allowed me to frame the problem as:


How might we help those struggling to build a wardrobe they are comfortable with in an enjoyable and personalized manner, while allowing customers to feel confident about their style and choices?

SOLUTION

Exploratory and Mission based shoppers.

What I aim to do with this iteration is to allow exploratory users to discover new items of clothing and feel confident about their choices due to the readily available information. However, there is also the choice for mission based customers that know what they are looking for to do so efficiently.

THEMES EXPLORED

Personalizing content to build loyalty and trust in the brand.

Providing customer service to the level of in-person satisfaction.

WHY ARITZIA?

In-House Designs.

Aritzia is a Vancouver based clothing line that runs several retail stores across the Lower Mainland. Aritzia is unique in the sense that it does not carry any Aritzia brand clothing, instead they use in-house designers that make up the majority of the brands sold in Aritzia. For example, Babaton brand works towards minimalism that excels for work wear and classier nights out.


Avoiding Obsolete Trends.

Why we believed Aritzia was a great fit for an intervention was because of this unique aspect of their brand that wasn't showcased. The ability to be flexible utilizing their brands, Aritzia avoids obsolescence by cycling through trends and manipulating their stock accordingly. Customers are unaware of these trends and what Aritzia's brands are made for.
